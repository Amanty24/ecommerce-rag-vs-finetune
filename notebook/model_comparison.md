# Model Comparison: Fine-tuned Model vs. RAG Model

This document provides a comparison between the responses generated by the **Fine-tuned Model** and the **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) Model** for e-commerce customer support queries.

## Query Comparisons

### Q1: I live in a remote area. How long does it usually take for delivery?

**Fine-tuned Model:**
> It depends on the distance between the delivery location and the nearest transportation hub. Generally, deliveries are made within a few days to a week depending on the distance and the current situation, such as COVID-19 restrictions or other factors. However, it's always a good idea to check with the delivery company or the local authorities for the latest updates on delivery times, especially during crisis situations.

**RAG Model:**
> The delivery timeline for remote areas may vary depending on the location and product availability. Metro deliveries usually take 2-4 days, while remote areas may take up to 10 days. However, the exact timeline may vary depending on the courier service and logistics sync issues. Customers are advised to check with neighbors or contact support for updates.

**Observations:**
* The **Fine-tuned model** provides a generic, somewhat outdated response (mentioning COVID-19 restrictions) without specific timelines for the business.
* The **RAG model** gives precise, policy-based information (10 days for remote areas) which is much more useful for a customer.

---

### Q2: Can I exchange a product instead of returning it?

**Fine-tuned Model:**
> Yes, you can exchange or return a product instead of returning it as long as the product is in the same condition as when you received it and is unused. You can either contact the retailer directly or visit their customer service center to initiate the exchange or return process. Make sure to follow the store's return or exchange policy and procedures for proper handling and packaging.

**RAG Model:**
> Yes, you can exchange a product instead of returning it. Exchanges are available for size issues or defective items. Replacement depends on stock availability.

**Observations:**
* The **Fine-tuned model** uses redundant phrasing ("exchange or return a product instead of returning it") and gives generic "visit the retailer" advice.
* The **RAG model** is concise and correctly identifies the conditions for exchange (size issues, defective items).

---

### Q3: I lost the original packaging. Can I still return the product?

**Fine-tuned Model:**
> Yes, you can still return the product if it's in its original packaging and the product is in its original condition. However, please ensure that the returns are made within 14 days of receiving the product. The return policy of the seller may include specific requirements for returning the product. It's best to check the seller's return policy and contact them for more information.

**RAG Model:**
> Yes, you can still return the product if you lost the original packaging. However, you will need to follow the return process as mentioned in the Knowledge section.

**Observations:**
* The **Fine-tuned model** contradicts itself by saying "yes you can return if you lost it... if it's in its original packaging". It also hallucinates a 14-day return window.
* The **RAG model** correctly addresses the specific concern about lost packaging and refers to the established process.

---

### Q4: I want to merge two accounts. Is that possible?

**Fine-tuned Model:**
> Yes, that is possible. You can merge two accounts using the `merger` utility in the `gimp` command-line utility. 
> 1. Open the `gimp` command-line utility on your computer.
> 2. Type `merger` into the command line.
> 3. Enter the account names separated by a comma or space...

**RAG Model:**
> Yes, it is possible to merge two accounts. However, there are some limitations and requirements that need to be met.
> 1. The accounts must be in the same country.
> 2. The accounts must have the same email address.
> 3. The accounts must have the same billing and shipping addresses.
> 4. The accounts must have the same payment methods.
> 5. The accounts must have the same currency.

**Observations:**
* The **Fine-tuned model** completely hallucinates a technical solution involving `gimp` (an image editor) for account merging, which is dangerously incorrect.
* The **RAG model** provides a realistic, structured list of business requirements for account merging.

---

### Q5: I want to use my loyalty points for a ₹900 order, but it's not working. Why?

**Fine-tuned Model:**
> Since you are not able to use your loyalty points for a ₹900 order, I can suggest why this might be the case:
> 1. The order value that you are trying to place is not available for redemption. The loyalty points you have earned are only valid for a certain number of purchases, and you have exceeded the limit for that order.
> 2. The loyalty points you have earned are inactive.

**RAG Model:**
> Loyalty points are not directly associated with the order value. They are a reward system that customers can earn by making purchases on the website. The loyalty points are then redeemed for discounts, free shipping, or other rewards. However, the order value is determined based on the total price of the items in the cart. If the order value is less than the loyalty points value, the loyalty points will not be applied to the order.

**Observations:**
* The **Fine-tuned model** invents arbitrary rules and limits to explain the failure, which could mislead the customer.
* The **RAG model** attempts to explain the logic of the loyalty system based on available data, remaining grounded in the concept of redemption rules.

---

## Conclusion

Based on the responses above, we can draw the following conclusions:
1. **Accuracy:** The RAG model is significantly more accurate because it fetches actual business rules (like the remote area delivery timeline), whereas the Fine-Tuned model hallucinates policies and even technical solutions.
2. **Concision:** The RAG model provides direct, actionable answers. The Fine-Tuned model is often verbose and contains redundant or contradictory information.
3. **Reliability:** For customer support, the RAG model proves to be much more reliable, as it doesn't give customers fake instructions (like the `gimp` hallucination in Q4).
